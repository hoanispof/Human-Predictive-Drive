  CONTEXT: 13M thất nghiệp, gần nửa ngân hàng sụp, bank runs.
    Fear đang TỰ gây ra economic collapse (self-fulfilling prophecy).

  PREDECESSORS (400+ years):

    Francis Bacon (1623): "Nil terribile nisi ipse timor."
    Thoreau (1851): "Nothing is so much to be feared as fear."


⭐ QUOTE 2 — JUNG CONCEPT:

  MAINSTREAM: "What you resist, persists." — Carl Jung

  THỰC TẾ: Quote này KHÔNG XUẤT HIỆN trong Collected Works, thư,
    hay bài giảng nào của Jung. Viktor Frankl Institute xác nhận.

  NGUỒN THẬT SỰ:
    Florence Scovel Shinn (1925, The Game of Life):
      "Whatever we resist, persists" — via "law of non-resistance."
    Werner Erhard (1970s, est training): popularized tới đại chúng.

  JUNG THỰC SỰ VIẾT (Aion, 1951):
    "The shadow is a moral problem that challenges the whole
     ego-personality, for no one can become conscious of the shadow
     without considerable moral effort."
    + Unconscious forces "rendered harmless if made CONSCIOUS."

  SAI LỆCH QUAN TRỌNG:
    Mainstream: "đừng resist" → gợi ý PASSIVE acceptance
    Jung actual: "actively confront and integrate" → INTENSE process
    "Confrontation with the unconscious" (1913-1930) = ferociously intense


⭐ QUOTE 3 — FRANKL/MAY:

  MAINSTREAM: "Between stimulus and response there is a space.
    In that space is our power to choose our response.
    In our response lies our growth and our freedom." — Viktor Frankl

  THỰC TẾ: Viktor Frankl Institute CHÍNH THỨC xác nhận:
    quote KHÔNG có trong bất kỳ tác phẩm nào của Frankl.

  NGUỒN THẬT SỰ:
    Rollo May (1963, "Freedom and Responsibility Re-Examined"):
      "Man has the capacity for pause between stimulus and response —
       he may choose among alternatives in responding."
    Rollo May (1975, The Courage to Create): expanded.
    Stephen Covey (1989, 7 Habits): đọc ở Hawaii, đặt cạnh Frankl → misattribute.

  FRANKL THỰC SỰ VIẾT (Man's Search for Meaning, 1946):
    "Everything can be taken from a man but one thing: the last of
     the human freedoms — to choose one's ATTITUDE in any given set
     of circumstances, to choose one's own way."

  KHÁC BIỆT QUAN TRỌNG:
    Misquote: "choose response" = behavior (external output)
    Frankl actual: "choose ATTITUDE" = direction (internal orientation)
    Frankl: "LAST freedom" = khi TẤT CẢ bị mất, còn lại cái này
    Misquote: "space" gợi ý universal, available to all
    Frankl: "last freedom" thừa nhận mọi thứ khác ĐÃ MẤT


⭐ QUOTE 4 — NIETZSCHE:

  MAINSTREAM: "What doesn't kill me makes me stronger."

  ORIGINAL (Twilight of the Idols, 1888, Sprüche und Pfeile #8):
    "Aus der Kriegsschule des Lebens. —
     Was mich nicht umbringt, macht mich stärker."
    = "Từ TRƯỜNG CHIẾN TRANH của đời sống. —
       Cái không giết được tôi sẽ làm tôi mạnh hơn."

  PHẦN BỊ CẮT:
    "Trường chiến tranh" → Nietzsche mô tả thái độ CHIẾN ĐẤU với tồn tại,
    không phải lời khuyên trị liệu hay motivational poster.

  NIETZSCHE BIẾT SUFFERING CÓ THỂ PHÁ HỦY:
    Ecce Homo: "the instinct of self-recovery forbade my holding
    to a philosophy of poverty and desperation."
    = ASPIRATIONAL (lý tưởng người mạnh hướng tới), KHÔNG universal law.


⭐ QUOTE 5 — MARCUS AURELIUS:

  MAINSTREAM: "The obstacle is the way."

  THỰC TẾ: Phrase này KHÔNG CÓ trong Meditations.
    Ryan Holiday (2014) paraphrase → popularize.

  ORIGINAL (Meditations 5.20, Koine Greek):
    "Our actions may be impeded, but there can be no impeding our
     intentions or dispositions, because we can accommodate and adapt.
     The mind adapts and converts to its own purposes the obstacle
     to our acting. The impediment to action advances action.
     What stands in the way becomes the way."

  PHẦN BỊ COMPRESS:
    Marcus nói 3 steps: (a) external CAN block action,
    (b) CANNOT block intention/disposition,
    (c) mind CONVERTS hindrance → material for NEW action.
    Mainstream giữ kết luận, bỏ TOÀN BỘ lập luận.

  STOIC CONTEXT:
    Epictetus (Discourses 1.1): "things in our power" vs "not in our power"
    Seneca (Letters 78.13): suffering worsened by opinion, reframing restores agency
    Marcus applied → governance under plague, war, betrayal.
