## В§2 вҖ” TAU: FROM NORMAL PROTEIN TO DISEASE

### В§2.1 вҖ” Normal Tau: The Cross-Ties Holding the Railroad

```
рҹҹў TAU = MICROTUBULE STABILIZER:

  Gene: MAPT (Microtubule-Associated Protein Tau)
    вҶ’ Chromosome 17q21
    вҶ’ 6 isoforms via alternative splicing
    вҶ’ Divided into 3R (3 repeat) and 4R (4 repeat domains)
    вҶ’ 4R tau stabilizes microtubules MORE STRONGLY than 3R
    вҶ’ Size: 48-67 kDa (varies by isoform)

  Normal function:
    вҶ’ Stabilizes microtubules = internal "skeleton" of neuron
    вҶ’ Microtubules = "railroad tracks" for transport:
      ATP, neurotransmitters, vesicles, mitochondria
      вҶ’ From cell body вҶ’ synapse (anterograde, kinesin)
      вҶ’ From synapse вҶ’ cell body (retrograde, dynein)
    вҶ’ Tau = "cross-tie" keeping the tracks straight + stable

  вӯҗ UNEVEN DISTRIBUTION in brain:
    вҶ’ Most concentrated in: locus coeruleus, entorhinal cortex,
      hippocampal neurons, cholinergic basal forebrain
    вҶ’ = EXACTLY the neurons most vulnerable in Alzheimer
    вҶ’ NOT random: neurons with LONG axons + HIGH metabolic demand
      вҶ’ need more microtubule stabilization вҶ’ more tau
      вҶ’ more tau вҶ’ more risk of hyperphosphorylation
```

### В§2.2 вҖ” Tau Phosphorylation: Normal vs Pathological

```
рҹҹў PHOSPHORYLATION = THE KEY:

  вӯҗ NORMAL:
    вҶ’ 2-3 mol phosphate / mol tau protein
    вҶ’ 85 potential phosphorylation sites
    вҶ’ Phosphorylation = NORMAL, CONTINUOUS process
    вҶ’ Regulates tau-microtubule binding strength
    вҶ’ Necessary for synaptic plasticity (see В§2.5)

  вӯҗ PATHOLOGICAL (Alzheimer):
    вҶ’ 3-4Г— normal level (range 5-9 mol/mol typical)
    вҶ’ ~10 mol/mol at threshold for filament formation
    вҶ’ >40 sites confirmed hyperphosphorylated
    вҶ’ Tau LEAVES microtubule вҶ’ microtubule COLLAPSE
    вҶ’ Free tau вҶ’ oligomerize вҶ’ fibril вҶ’ tangle

  "GAS INCREASES + BRAKES FAIL":
    Gas = KINASES (add phosphate):
      вҶ’ GSK-3ОІ (glycogen synthase kinase 3ОІ): MAIN kinase
      вҶ’ CDK5 (cyclin-dependent kinase 5): kinase #2
    Brakes = PHOSPHATASES (remove phosphate):
      вҶ’ PP2A = ~71% total phosphatase activity in the brain
        (Liu et al. 2005, European Journal of Neuroscience)
      вҶ’ PP2A CLEARLY REDUCED in Alzheimer cortex + hippocampus
        (Gong et al. 1993, Journal of Neurochemistry)

  AGING makes it worse:
    CDK5 in youth: INHIBITS GSK-3ОІ (secondary brake)
    CDK5 in old age: LOSES inhibition вҶ’ GSK-3ОІ RUNS FREE
    вҶ’ Gas INCREASES (GSK-3ОІ freed) + Brakes FAIL (PP2A reduced)
    вҶ’ = Hyperphosphorylation increases with age = INEVITABLE part
```

### В§2.3 вҖ” Progression: Pretangle вҶ’ Oligomer вҶ’ Fibril вҶ’ Tangle

```
рҹҹў 5 STAGES OF TAU PATHOLOGY PROGRESSION:

  в‘  PRETANGLE:
    вҶ’ Tau hyperphosphorylated but NOT yet aggregated into fibril
    вҶ’ Predominantly 4R tau isoform
    вҶ’ Neuron STILL healthy, function STILL normal
    вҶ’ REVERSIBLE: if phosphatases recover вҶ’ tau rebinds microtubule
    вҶ’ Arendt et al. 2003 (J Neuroscience): hibernation analogy
      вҶ’ Hibernating animals: MASSIVE tau hyperphosphorylation
      вҶ’ Upon waking: PP2A recovers вҶ’ tau rebinds вҶ’ NORMAL
      вҶ’ = Tau hyperphosphorylation ITSELF вү  disease

  в‘Ў OLIGOMERS = MOST TOXIC:
    вҶ’ Tau monomers aggregate into small clusters (2-50+ monomers)
    вҶ’ SOLUBLE: moves freely in cytoplasm
    вҶ’ Niewiadomska et al. 2021 (Life, MDPI 11(1):28):
      oligomeric tau = the MOST CYTOTOXIC form
    вҶ’ Damages: disrupts synapses, impairs mitochondria,
      damages membranes, spreads trans-synaptically
    вҶ’ CAN reverse вҶ’ but VERY DIFFICULT at this stage
    вҶ’ вӯҗ Aggregation into fibrils = paradoxically may be
      "cell rescue mechanism" вҖ” reduced solubility вҶ’ reduced toxicity

  в‘ў FIBRILS (PHF вҖ” Paired Helical Filaments):
    вҶ’ 2 protofilaments twisted around each other, ~20nm diameter
    вҶ’ INSOLUBLE: does not move freely вҶ’ accumulates in place
    вҶ’ Less toxic than oligomers (already "locked up")
    вҶ’ BUT: destroys microtubule structure, blocks transport
    вҶ’ DIFFICULT to naturally reverse

  в‘Ј TANGLE (Neurofibrillary Tangle вҖ” NFT):
    вҶ’ PHFs accumulate into tangle INSIDE neuron (intracellular)
    вҶ’ Size: 5-15 Ојm (much larger than organelles)
    вҶ’ Neuron "crushed" вҶ’ function COLLAPSES COMPLETELY
    вҶ’ IRREVERSIBLE

  в‘Ө GHOST TANGLE:
    вҶ’ Neuron HAS DIED вҶ’ tangle escapes outside (extracellular)
    вҶ’ Persists MANY YEARS after neuron death
    вҶ’ Isoform shift: 4R (early pretangle) вҶ’ 3R (late ghost tangle)
    вҶ’ = Neuron's "gravestone" вҖ” tau trace PERSISTS after host is gone

  INSIGHT: Braak staging (В§4) measures WHERE tangles are вҶ’ maps progression
```

### В§2.4 вҖ” Tau Begins in CHILDHOOD: Universal + Harmless

```
рҹҹў BRAAK & DEL TREDICI 2011 (Acta Neuropathol 121(2):171-181):

    вҶ’ 38/42 (90.5%) already had pretangle tau in locus coeruleus
    вҶ’ 41/42 had NO amyloid
    вҶ’ = Tau pathology begins IN CHILDHOOD, without amyloid

    вҶ’ By age 40: nearly 100% have pretangle tau
    вҶ’ By age 80: nearly 100% have tau at least Braak II

  вӯҗ FRAMEWORK CRITICAL INSIGHT:
    вҶ’ Pretangle tau = UNIVERSAL вҖ” metabolic byproduct, NOT random mutation
    вҶ’ HARMLESS in most people: cleanup (PP2A, proteasome) can handle it

  вҡ пёҸ IMPORTANT вҖ” Tau pathology IS DIFFERENT FROM:
    вҶ’ Pruning (developmental synaptic pruning): planned, healthy, developmental
```

### В§6.3 вҖ” Mechanism 3: Activity-Dependent Tau Spread

```

    вҶ’ Recent connections = ACTIVELY USED вҶ’ release MORE tau вҶ’ destroyed FIRST
```

### В§6.4 вҖ” Mechanism 4: Multiple Trace Theory

```
рҹҹў NADEL & MOSCOVITCH 1997 (Current Opinion in Neurobiology 7(2):217-227):

  EACH TIME a memory is recalled = creates a NEW trace:
    вҶ’ Old memory recalled 100 times вҶ’ 100+ traces (many brain regions)
    вҶ’ Recent memory recalled 2 times вҶ’ 2 traces (concentrated)

  вҶ’ Partial damage:
    вҶ’ Old memory: destroy 20 traces вҶ’ STILL HAS 80 вҶ’ SURVIVE
    вҶ’ Recent memory: destroy 1 trace вҶ’ ONLY 1 LEFT вҶ’ VULNERABLE
    вҶ’ = Old memories tolerate MORE damage

  = Retrieval strengthening effect:
    вҶ’ Each recall = adds 1 "copy" in a different brain region
    вҶ’ Multiple copies = protection against focal damage
    вҶ’ DIFFERENT from just "strengthening" (same trace gets stronger)
    вҶ’ = ADDS NEW traces (multiple, distributed)
```

```

    вҶ’ Brainstem вҶ’ motor вҶ’ sensory (EARLY, infancy вҶ’ age 6)
    вҶ’ Hippocampus вҶ’ PFC вҶ’ association cortex (LATE, age 6 вҶ’ 25+)

  Late-myelinating = VULNERABLE FIRST in Alzheimer:
    вҶ’ Myelin = "insulating sheath" around axons
    вҶ’ Late-myelinating regions: THINNER myelin + DETERIORATES EARLIER
    вҶ’ = Exactly the regions Alzheimer attacks FIRST

    вҶ’ Early-myelinating regions: THICK + ROBUST myelin вҶ’ resists Alzheimer LAST
    вҶ’ = Exactly the regions Alzheimer attacks LAST
```
