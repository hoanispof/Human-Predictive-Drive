### §1.1 — ① Social media drain vs conversation bạn thân

```
STATUS: KEEP + REFINE ("+bạn thân")

SOURCES: Connection.md v5.0, Inter-Body-Mechanism.md §1 (③ Social Hardware)
RESEARCH: Coan 2015 (Social Baseline Theory), Holt-Lunstad 2015,
          Tronick 1978, Eisenberger 2003
POST ROLE: EXPANDED mechanism sketch (lead example)
```

#### Mechanism Chain

```
SOCIAL MEDIA (1 hour):
  Step 1: Feed delivers novelty → VTA fires dopamine
          = "something prediction-relevant changed" (Schultz 1997)
  Step 2: Body checks: contingent responsiveness? → NO
          (feed doesn't react to YOU specifically)
  Step 3: Body checks: multi-sensory presence? → NO
          (no voice tone, no facial micro-expressions, no proximity)
  Step 4: Body checks: co-regulation? → NO
          (no cortisol buffering through trusted presence)
  Step 5: Dopamine (wanting) fires repeatedly → attention captured
  Step 6: Opioid system (liking) → NOT firing
          (requires depth + contingent responsiveness + presence)
  Step 7: After 1 hour: high alertness (dopamine), low nourishment (no opioid)
          = "socially unfed" state despite high engagement

CONVERSATION WITH CLOSE FRIEND (30 min):
  Step 1: Trusted entity (Entity-Access Mức 3-4) → Hardware-Subsidy ACTIVE
          (oxytocin auto-release for compiled entity)
  Step 2: Contingent responsiveness: friend reacts to YOU, real-time
  Step 3: Multi-sensory: voice tone, facial expression, physical proximity
  Step 4: Co-regulation: cortisol buffering (Coan 2015 Social Baseline Theory)
          Body DEFAULT state = with others. Alone = deviation.
  Step 5: μ-opioid fires genuine reward (social play pathway, Panksepp 1998)
  Step 6: Oxytocin modulates cortisol → "social buffering" → body relaxes
  Step 7: After 30 min: genuinely nourished, cortisol lower, opioid satisfied

CORE DISTINCTION:
  Dopamine = "something changed" (wanting/alertness)
  Opioid   = "something matched a body-level need" (liking/satisfaction)
  MXH fires wanting WITHOUT liking → body's assessment: depleted
  Close friend fires BOTH → body's assessment: nourished

WHAT "BẠN THÂN" ADDS vs generic "real conversation":
  Close friend = Entity-Compiled deep (Mức 3-4)
  → Hardware-Subsidy ACTIVE: oxytocin auto-release, μ-opioid primed
  → Self-Pattern-Modeling library DEEP: predict each other accurately
  → History chunks: shared experiences = structural valence
  Stranger conversation = STILL better than MXH
  (contingent responsiveness + presence present)
  BUT lacks Hardware-Subsidy → less oxytocin, less co-regulation
  → "Bạn thân" = STRONGEST contrast with MXH

FALSIFICATION:
  "If body shows identical opioid response to social media
   as to face-to-face conversation → framework breaks."
  "If removing contingent responsiveness from conversation
   doesn't reduce opioid response → framework breaks."
```

#### English Formulation

```
QUESTION (for post):
  "Why does scrolling social media for an hour leave you drained —
   when a 30-minute conversation with a close friend energizes you?"

MECHANISM SKETCH (expanded, ~150 words):

  The framework identifies 8 pathways through which the body registers
  connection — including co-regulation (cortisol buffering through trusted
  presence), contingent responsiveness (the other person reacts to *you*,
  in real time), and multi-sensory presence signal.

  A conversation with a close friend activates most of these simultaneously.
  The body detects a trusted entity — oxytocin modulates cortisol
  ("social buffering" — Coan 2015), and the opioid system fires genuine
  reward. A close friend means deeper: compiled trust, shared history,
  hardware-subsidy active.

  Social media activates 3–4 pathways at surface level only. The critical
  missing element: body-level presence. Without voice tone, facial
  micro-expressions, and contingent response, the body stays in a
  "socially unfed" state despite high engagement.

  The core distinction: dopamine tracks "something prediction-relevant
  changed" (wanting); opioid tracks "something genuinely matched a
  body-level need" (liking). Social media fires the first without the
  second. The body's verdict after an hour: high alertness, low nourishment.

  Source: Connection.md v5.0.
  Research: Coan 2015, Holt-Lunstad 2015, Tronick 1978.
```

### §1.5 — ⑤ Creative stress + when harmful

```
STATUS: KEEP + EXPAND ("+khi nào có hại")
TIER: ★★★★
SOURCES: Cortisol-Baseline.md v2.1 (7 modes, Inverted-U, direction),
         Body-Feedback-Mechanism.md v2.0 (gap detection)
RESEARCH: Yerkes-Dodson (Inverted-U), Reyes et al. 2020 (n=46, cortisol ≠ stress)
POST ROLE: BRIEF mechanism sketch (expand from v1)
```

#### Mechanism Chain

```
WHY CREATIVE WORK = STRESS (by design):
  Step 1: Creativity = opening gaps in your model (prediction-delta)
  Step 2: Gap detected → cortisol mobilizes
          = change-readiness AMPLIFIER, not damage signal
  Step 3: PFC Fresh processing (drafting novel solutions,
          holding options, suppressing premature closure)
          costs real metabolic resources: glucose, PFC slots, sustained cortisol
  Step 4: Inverted-U explains sweet spot:
          Enough cortisol → signal urgency, mobilize resources
          Not enough → no mobilization, drift
          Too much → PFC locked into threat mode, narrow processing

WHEN IT BECOMES HARMFUL:
  Condition 1: SOURCE DIRECTION = THREAT (not creative approach)
    → Creative approach cortisol: "gap is interesting, mobilize to explore"
    → Deadline panic cortisol: "gap is threatening, mobilize to escape"
    → Same molecule, different what-it-mobilizes-for
    → Threat direction → PFC narrows → LESS creative, not more

  Condition 2: SUSTAINED WITHOUT REPAIR
    → Cortisol → cortisol → cortisol (no rest, no completion, no reward)
    → HPA axis dysregulation (Cortisol-Baseline.md: vicious cycle)
    → Body shifts from "mobilized" to "depleted"
    → = Burnout territory

  Condition 3: EXCEEDS INVERTED-U PEAK
    → Past peak: cortisol LOCKS PFC into threat mode
    → Working memory narrows, creativity drops
    → "Trying harder" = more cortisol = WORSE performance
    → = "Creative block under pressure" = Inverted-U in action

FRAMEWORK POSITION:
  Mainstream: "reduce cortisol = reduce stress"
  Framework: cortisol = amplifier. Zero cortisol = medical emergency (Addison's).
  What matters: source direction + repair balance, not level.
  Injecting cortisol doesn't produce stress (Reyes et al. 2020).

FALSIFICATION:
  "If injecting cortisol reliably produces subjective stress
   independent of context → framework's 'amplifier not cause' breaks."
  (Reyes 2020 already shows it doesn't)
```

```

  PFC narrative "I tried hard" = irrelevant to body-feedback

  ① NOT just "fast = bias, slow = rational"
```

```
MECHANISM SKETCH (expanded, ~180 words):

  The brain compiles knowledge through experience. Repeated exposure
  to domain feedback — debugging code, tasting food, reading patients —
  builds body-level patterns that fire automatically: near-instant,
  near-zero cost. This is what Klein (1998) documented as

  recognize patterns.

  general-purpose, costly). Every programmer knows the pattern: the senior

  hours. Same codebase. Different compiled base.

  But compiled doesn't mean correct — it means untested assumptions
  feel identical to verified expertise. Domain feedback is the only
  arbiter. The body rewards results, not effort.
```
