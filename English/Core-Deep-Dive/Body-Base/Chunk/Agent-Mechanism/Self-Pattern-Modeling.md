> Self-Pattern-Modeling is truly forward simulation (build model + run + predict), not pattern-matching.

> You see your close friend crying. Your body feels slightly sad — EVEN THOUGH nothing happened to you.
> That is Compiled: body SIMULATES the other person's state — automatic, nearly unconscious.
>

> That is Fresh: PFC CHAINS a draft prediction of next behavior — deliberate, costs effort.
>

> "Feeling" and "Logic"? Just LABELS — Einstein "felt" math because math was COMPILED.
> A new therapist "thinks about" emotion because the case is still FRESH.
> The real axis: COMPILED ←→ FRESH. Content doesn't decide it.
>
> And EVERYTHING depends on Per-Agent Valence — how does the body evaluate THIS agent?
> Positive valence: Compiled = empathy. Negative valence: Compiled = REVERSED (pleasant when the enemy suffers).
> Same mechanism, same 6 steps — different output because different valence.

Self-Pattern-Modeling is the mechanism by which you use yourself as a template to model other entities — APPLICATION-1 on Simulation-Engine (Other × Present × Simulate). Self-Pattern-Modeling is 1 mechanism across 3 dimensions (Processing Level × Valence Direction × Output Intensity), not 4 separate skills.

```
⭐ SELF-PATTERN-MODELING FIRES WHEN THE TARGET HAS "AGENT PROPERTIES":

     → Friend sad → behaves DIFFERENTLY than when happy → Self-Pattern-Modeling must simulate state.

     → Mammals: shared neurotransmitters → Compiled CAN fire.
```
