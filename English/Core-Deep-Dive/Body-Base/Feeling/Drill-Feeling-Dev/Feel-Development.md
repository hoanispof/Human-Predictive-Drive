## §1 — Expanded Feeling Definition

[Phase 4 fill]

### §1.1 — Original vs Refined

**Original** (Feeling.md): "body-base feedback system"

**Refined**: "Feeling = everything PFC can observe from body-base or
from unconscious processing — whether or not it can be verbalized or
clearly defined. Called 'feeling' because not all of it is verbalizable.
Detection capacity depends on how developed the PFC is."

### §1.2 — Expansion reasoning

Body-base alone is TOO NARROW. Examples that fit the expanded definition
but not the original:
- Metacognitive (sense of knowing a math problem will resolve)
- Cognitive confidence (certainty about an argument)
- Social intuition (sensing someone is upset before they say so)
- Aesthetic feeling (diffuse beauty response)
- Predictive interoception (need-to-urinate signal before urgency peaks)
- Felt sense (Gendlin's pre-verbal body wisdom)

### §1.3 — Unified mechanism

ALL are PFC observing unconscious processing, whether:
- Body (interoception)
- Schemas (pattern matching)
- Predictions (interoceptive + exteroceptive forecasts)
- Memory retrieval (sense of remembering)
- Inference (sense of conclusion forming)
- Simulator (Resonance output)
- Integration (diffuse multi-source states)

This unifies fields previously treated separately: interoception research,
metacognition, felt sense theory, somatic marker hypothesis, social cognition,
and aesthetics — all under one mechanism.

## §2 — PFC as Observer (Not Executor)

[Phase 4 fill]

### §2.1 — The role

PFC has READ ACCESS to integrated upstream signals. It does NOT execute
body processes, schemas, predictions, or simulations. Those run elsewhere.

### §2.2 — What PFC does
- Observe (read integrated signals)
- Label (via chunks)
- Integrate (combine multiple channel reads)
- Plan (strategic reasoning)
- Report (verbalize when chunks support it)

### §2.3 — What PFC does NOT do
- Execute interoception (body does this)
- Run schemas (cortex does this)
- Generate feelings (unconscious processing generates them)
- Directly access raw body signals (integration happens upstream)

### §2.4 — 7-layer structure refined

- Feel-RawSignals / Feel-Integration: upstream integration (PFC has no direct access)
- Feel-Consciousification / Feel-Observation: PFC's read interface (genuine "felt sense")
- Feel-Location / Feel-Explanation: chunk-based labeling (error-prone)

This partially resolves the Logic-Feeling flow question:
- NOT Model A (PFC directly senses raw body signals)
- NOT Model B (PFC is pure external labeler)
- IS: PFC reads integrated mid-level signals and labels via chunks

### §2.5 — Alignments
- Predictive Processing: PFC tracks prediction errors, not generates them
- Damasio: "feeling of what happens"
- Gendlin: felt sense
- Buddhist mindfulness: consciousness as observer, not actor

## §4 — Multi-Axis Development Model

[Phase 4 fill — this is the CORE section of this file]

### §4.1 — Why multi-axis, not single linear stages

Single-track stage models are too simplistic. Reality: individuals have
**profile vectors** — deep on some channels, shallow on others, varying in
speed, integration capacity, and action coupling.

### §4.2 — The 5 axes

| Axis | Name | Range | Mechanism |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Depth per channel | Shallow ↔ Deep | Chunk library richness within a single channel |
| 2 | Breadth across channels | Narrow ↔ Broad | Number of channels accessible |
| 3 | Speed / Automaticity | Effortful ↔ Automatic | Practice converts effortful to automatic |
| 4 | Integration capacity | Single ↔ Multi-channel | Simultaneous observation capability |
| 5 | Action coupling | Disconnected ↔ Tight | Observation → response translation quality |

Possible 6th axis: Meta-awareness (awareness of awareness itself)

### §4.3 — Profile vectors: real-world examples

- **Olympic Athlete**: deep body, shallow emotional, fast body reactions,
  single-channel focus, tight body-action coupling
- **Long-term Meditator**: deep body + emotional + meta, broad, variable speed,
  high integration, moderate coupling
- **Successful Therapist**: deep social + emotional, broad, fast social reading,
  high integration, tight helping coupling
- **Pure Intellectual**: deep cognitive / metacognitive, often shallow body
  and emotional channels, fast cognitive, variable integration
- **Alexithymic**: shallow Axis 1 across the board, particularly emotional
- **Balanced Developer**: moderate across many channels (natural scaffolded child)
- **Trauma Survivor**: distorted profile, suppression patterns active

### §4.4 — Specialization vs Balance

Most experts spike 1–2 channels. Time and practice constraints make
extreme depth across all channels impossible. Society needs both profiles.

### §4.5 — Blindspot generation

Going deep on one channel → systematic blindspots on others. This is not
pathology — it is a byproduct of resource allocation. Examples:
- Athlete who cannot read social cues
- Therapist disconnected from their own body
- Intellectual without emotional access
- CEO who ignores fatigue signals

### §4.6 — Cross-channel scaffolding

Similar channels transfer. "Knowing when you need to urinate →
makes knowing when you need a bowel movement easier." But transfer
is imperfect — depth in channel X does not automatically transfer to Y.

## §5 — Math Formula Analogy

[Phase 4 fill]

### §5.1 — The analogy

Chunks for feeling detection are like math formulas in their structure:
- Counting (Stage 0)
- Addition and subtraction (Stage 1)
- Multiplication and division (Stage 2)
- Algebra (Stage 3)
- Trigonometry / Calculus (specialization branches)
- Quantum mechanics (deep specialization)

### §5.2 — Shared properties
- Cumulative: foundation is required before advancement
- Branching: multiple specialization paths are possible
- Specialization possible without mastering every branch
- Most people stop at intermediate levels
- Some go very deep in specific domains
- Cross-branch knowledge is possible but not automatic
- Each person's progression chain differs

### §5.3 — Applied to feeling chunks
- Distress / comfort → discriminate types → verbal labels → complex
  emotions → Resonance theory of mind → metacognitive feeling → existential
- Similar branching: body-deep vs social-deep vs cognitive-deep
- Each feeling domain = its own specialization tree

### §5.4 — Implications
- Feeling literacy can be TAUGHT (exactly as math can be taught)
- Foundation must be built first (cannot skip stages)
- Specialization is possible (don't need everything)
- Most people stop at functional level
- Expert literacy is achievable with deliberate training
