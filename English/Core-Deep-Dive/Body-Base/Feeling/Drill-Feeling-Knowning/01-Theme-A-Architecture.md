The framework has **two tiers of definition** currently coexisting:

**Tier 2** ([Feel-Development.md](../Feel-Development.md) §1 OUTLINE, 2026-04-14):
> "Feeling = **everything PFC can observe from body-base or from unconscious

**Unified mechanism** (Tier 2):

**The tension resolved**: Tier 1 tried to list all sources inside "body systems" —

### §2.2 — The Body-First Invariant (PRESERVED unchanged)

**This part is stable across both Tier 1 and Tier 2**: Body → Feeling → PFC. The
order is NEVER reversed.

**4 lines of evidence from Feeling-Accuracy.md §1**:

1. **Libet-style research** 🟢 (interpretation requires caveat): Unconscious brain
   activity precedes conscious decision by 300–500ms. PFC "decides" AFTER the body
   has prepared action.

2. **Damasio VMPFC patients** 🟢: Phineas Gage + modern cases. VMPFC damage → logic
   + memory + language INTACT, feeling-based decision LOST → paralysis. Iowa Gambling
   Task: normal subjects develop anticipatory SCR (skin conductance response) BEFORE
   consciously knowing which deck is bad. VMPFC patients: no anticipatory SCR, cannot
   learn.

3. **Interoception** 🟢 (Craig 2002, 2013): Afferent pathways body → brain run
   continuously. The insula integrates body state BEFORE cortical processing. PFC
   receives an ALREADY-INTEGRATED signal. **PFC does not access raw body signals
   directly.**

4. **Gendlin felt sense** 🟢: 50+ years of clinical observation. "Felt sense = body
   knowledge that is pre-verbal. Cannot be generated by thinking. Must be ACCESSED,
   not created."

**Implications** (from Feeling-Accuracy.md §1):
> "A. 'Think yourself into feeling' = **impossible**. You can trigger a schema that
> triggers body state (indirect path). But you cannot directly will a feeling into
> existence.
>
> B. PFC can INHIBIT the expression of feeling, not generate its source. Suppression
> works temporarily, but the source feeling continues at body level.
>
> C. Awareness matters: Body computes whether PFC notices or not. PFC attention =
> whether feeling becomes explicit. Not attending ≠ not happening."

**→ INVARIANT**: Regardless of which definition (Tier 1 or Tier 2), **body computes
first, feeling emerges, PFC observes after**. Theme A preserves this fully.

### §2.3 — Why "Body-Base" Alone Is Too Narrow (Evidence for Tier 2)

Feel-Development outline identifies 6 types of feelings that don't fit a purely
body-base frame:

**Example 1 — Metacognitive "feeling of knowing"**:
- Example from Feel-Example-Draft E78: "I know this problem is solvable"
- Source: memory retrieval + cognitive chunks
- Not body interoception — not body-base substrate in the traditional sense
- But IS a real feeling that PFC observes

**Example 2 — Cognitive confidence**:
- Example: "I feel confident about this argument" (E79)
- Source: schema fit + prediction coherence
- Body participates (mild arousal), but primary source is cognitive schemas

**Example 3 — Social intuition**:
- Example: "I feel this person can't be trusted" (E88)
- Source: Resonance output (simulator using own chunks)
- NOT pure body interoception — **the simulator is running other-state**

**Example 4 — Aesthetic feeling**:
- Example: "Artist notices when the painting 'works'" (E95)
- Source: compiled aesthetic chunks firing
- Diffuse body response, but core is pattern match at cognitive level

**Example 5 — Predictive interoception**:
- Example: Pre-urination signal before the urge peaks (E80)
- Source: **predictive processing** — body anticipates future state, not current state
- Craig 2009 "How do you feel — now?" extended to predictive interoception (Seth
  2013)

**Example 6 — Gendlin felt sense**:
- Pre-verbal body wisdom
- Source: integrated multi-channel compilation
- Body is source — but **what is integrated** includes schemas, memory, prediction,
  not just raw interoception

**Pattern across all 6**: All are real feelings PFC observes. None fit cleanly into
a "body-base" definition. All involve **unconscious processing beyond raw body
signals** — schemas, predictions, simulation, memory retrieval.

**Framework's own acknowledgment** (Feeling-Research.md §9 Research limits):
> "Computational models — Predictive processing (Friston, Seth) offers a framework.
> Interoceptive inference model (Seth 2013). **Relationship to framework not fully
> worked out. Promising convergence area.**"

→ The framework **knew** it needed to expand but hadn't committed yet. The
Feel-Development outline is the commitment.

### §2.5 — PFC's Role: Observer, Not Creator

Feel-Development.md §2 explicitly lists what PFC does and does not do:

**What PFC DOES**:
- **Observe** (read integrated upstream signals)
- **Label** (via chunks — error-prone at Feel-Labeling–Feel-Explanation)
- **Integrate** (combine multiple channel reads)
- **Plan** (strategic reasoning using chunks)
- **Report** (verbalize if chunks support)
- **Choose** between competing signals (executive function)
- **Direct attention** (top-down bias of what to observe)

**What PFC DOESN'T do**:
- **Execute interoception** (body does this)
- **Run schemas** (cortex runs them automatically)
- **Generate feelings** (unconscious processing does this)
- **Directly access raw body signals** (integration happens upstream)

**Imagine-Final.md §1 adds 3 specific PFC functions**:
1. **CONSCIOUSIFY**: name an already-existing unconscious Imagine-Final
2. **DOMAIN SIMULATE**: check feasibility and domain accuracy
3. **CHOOSE**: executive selection among competing Imagine-Finals

**PFC = REACTIVE, not proactive** (Imagine-Final §1):
> "PFC does NOT self-start → BODY CALLS PFC when needed. Body satisfied → PFC does
> not engage (autopilot). Body unsatisfied → dissonance signal → PFC ACTIVATES."

**The ~95/5 rule** (Anchor-Schema §1):
- ~95% of life: compiled schemas fire automatically → auto-anchor → PFC NOT involved
- ~5% of decisions: compiled schemas insufficient → PFC engages (new situations,
  conflicts, long-term decisions)

**→ Answer to Part 1 of user's question**: PFC does "observe the unconscious and
then choose" — **framework explicitly confirms**. But PFC does more than just observe
and choose:
- Observe (Feel-Consciousification–Feel-Observation integration)
- Label via chunks (Feel-Labeling, error-prone)
- Explain via narrative (Feel-Explanation, confabulation risk)
- Choose between competing signals (executive)
- Direct attention (top-down bias)

### §2.6 — "PFC Requests Unconscious to Compute or Hold for Chaining": User's
Hypothesis Check

User's question: **Is there a top-down "query mechanism" where PFC asks the
unconscious to continue computing or hold in order to chain?**

**Framework's position** (partial support, needs refinement):

**Direct evidence in framework**:
- Imagine-Final.md §1 Tier 2: PFC does perform "direct" operations — consciousify,
  domain-simulate, choose
- Imagine-Final.md §1.5 Phase 4 RELOAD: "body triggers PFC to re-engage with saved
  Imagine-Final" — top-down reload mechanism
- Anchor-Schema.md §1 Step ③: "PFC SCAN + IMAGINE: seek new anchor" — PFC actively
  generates candidates when compiled anchor is insufficient

**Indirect support from research**:
- **Global Workspace Theory** (Baars 1988) 🟢: PFC broadcast → whole-brain sync.
  This IS a top-down mechanism.
- **Predictive processing** (Clark 2013, Friston): PFC sets high-level priors →
  propagate down to sensory cortex. Top-down attention biases what the unconscious
  processes.
- **Miller & Cohen 2001** 🟢: PFC maintains goal representations → bias processing
  across the brain. Not pure passive observation.

**BUT**: The framework has not explicitly modeled **"PFC query → wait for response
→ chain"** as a mechanism.

**Closest framework concept — "Attention allocation"** (Feeling.md §2.5
Feel-Observation):
> "Feel-Observation requires PFC allocation (attention budget)."

**And Imagine-Final §1.5 BACKGROUND phase**:
> "'Forcing thought' is WORSE than 'letting it soak in'. Most of the 'evolution' of
> Imagine-Final happens in PHASE 3 (background) — NOT when PFC is actively working."

**Key insight**: The framework says PFC can **allocate attention** (top-down) but
**chain formation happens mostly in the background** (not active PFC work). So the
user's intuition is **partially correct**:

- **YES**: PFC can direct attention, trigger reloads, choose which simulation to run
- **NO** (or partial): PFC doesn't "query and wait actively" — instead, PFC sets
  attention, releases control to unconscious processing, receives refined result on
  reload

**Refined answer**: Framework architecture:
1. PFC sets attention / anchor / question
2. PFC **releases** to unconscious processing (Background Phase)
3. Unconscious consolidates, spreads activation, refines chunks via REM + DMN
4. When body triggers (dissonance) or PFC reloads (actively pulling), PFC receives
   result
5. If unfinished, loop continues

**This is CLOSE to user's "request unconscious to compute or hold"** but with
nuance: PFC does not stay active waiting. It disengages and reloads later.

**→ Theme A confirms user's hypothesis with refinement**: Top-down query exists
(attention allocation + anchor setting). Chain formation itself happens in BACKGROUND
phase (DMN, REM consolidation, spreading activation). PFC reloads to see progress.
The user's intuition captures the direction-setting aspect correctly, but may
overstate how "active" the wait is.

### §2.7 — Spectrum: Clear Signals → Fuzzy Imagined Layer

User describes a spectrum: "from PFC detecting basic body-base signals (hunger, clear
pain, clear joy...) to PFC detecting through a faint imagined layer in the
unconscious."

**Framework's decomposition of this spectrum**:

#### §2.7.1 — Feel-Consciousification Clarity Dimension (from Feeling.md §2)

The 7-layer structure applies to ALL feeling sources, not just body interoception:

| Layer | Clarity | Example — clear signal | Example — fuzzy signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| Feel-RawSignals | 100% body truth | Gastric signals | Schema partial fire |
| Feel-Integration | 95% | Insula integration | Multi-schema integration |
| Feel-Consciousification | 90% | "Hungry — clearly" | "Something is there..." |
| Feel-Observation | 85% | "I am hungry" | "I am feeling something" |
| Feel-Location | 70-90% | "Hunger in stomach" | "In the chest but faint" |
| Feel-Labeling | 40-80% | "Hungry" | "Uneasy" |
| Feel-Explanation | 20-70% | "Because I haven't eaten" | "Because... I don't know" |

**Clear signals (obvious hunger, clear pain)** reach Feel-Location–Feel-Labeling
reliably. Fuzzy signals stay at Feel-Consciousification–Feel-Observation.

**This is NOT two different mechanisms** — it is the **same observation interface**
applied to signals of different strength and source.

#### §2.7.2 — Source Dimension

From Feeling-Sources.md §0-§10, 10 channels produce feelings. They differ in:

- **Source strength**: L1 physiological (high, clear) vs. imagine-final preview
  (low, fuzzy)
- **Temporal stability**: L1 (clear and sustained over time) vs. cognitive prediction
  (flickering, unstable)
- **Compilation depth**: meaning channel (evaluative, deep compile) vs. novel
  cross-domain (no compile yet)

**"Clear hunger"**:
- Source: L1 interoception (gastric signals, ghrelin)
- Integration: clean — single-source dominant
- Feel-Consciousification fidelity: high
- PFC labels easily: "hungry"

**"Fuzzy imagine-final preview"**:
- Source: simulation of a future state
- Integration: weak — preview is a diluted version of "real" body response
- Feel-Consciousification fidelity: lower
- PFC labels with uncertainty: "I feel like this plan will work... maybe?"

**Same mechanism, different signal strength.** Framework handles both via the same
PFC observation interface.

#### §2.7.3 — Why "Fuzzy" Feels Different (Feel-Consciousification felt sense)

**Gendlin's felt sense** (Feeling.md §2.4):
> "Feel-Consciousification is the point where the body has finished computing — but
> PFC has NOT yet applied any label. 'Something', 'feels somehow', 'don't know but
> feeling...' = TRUTH already ready. PFC doesn't have words yet."

**Why some feelings feel "fuzzy"**:
1. **Source is upstream integration** (not a single raw signal) → no single word
   maps cleanly
2. **Multiple channels firing weakly** → compression is difficult
3. **PFC chunks insufficient** → no learned label for this specific pattern yet
4. **Novel combination** → schema has no pre-compiled label

**Framework position** (from Feel-Development §2.4):
> "Logic-Feeling flow partially resolved: NOT Model A (PFC directly senses raw body),
> NOT Model B (PFC is pure external labeler), IS: **PFC reads integrated middle-
> ground signals, labels via chunks**."

**→ Fuzzy ≠ a different mechanism.** Fuzzy = same PFC observation interface seeing a
signal that:
- Has been integrated (Feel-Integration–Feel-Consciousification) but
- Has no compiled Feel-Labeling label yet

**Training (meditation, Focusing)** = learning to trust Feel-Consciousification–
Feel-Observation without forcing premature Feel-Labeling labels.

### §2.8 — "Or Is There Yet Another Processing Mechanism?" — User's Third Question

User's third question: Is there another processing mechanism beyond "PFC observes
unconscious + chooses + requests"?

**Framework's answer**: **Possibly, but the framework commits to a unified model**
with bidirectional flow.

#### §2.8.1 — No Separate "Imagine Layer" Mechanism

Framework does NOT treat "imagine layer feelings" as a separate mechanism:
- Imagine-Final previews = body simulation firing the same pathways as real experience
  (Schema-Operations §8.5, cited in Feeling.md)
- Mirror "copy" = own body firing a weaker version of the other's state (Gallese
  embodied simulation)
- Schema-triggered = body fires as if receiving real input (Feeling-Sources §7)

**Common pattern**: Body fires same pathways at different intensities, from different
triggers. PFC observes the integrated result. ONE mechanism, many inputs.

#### §2.8.2 — Bidirectional Flow Is an Architectural Complement

Anchor-Schema.md §0 adds the **bottom-up complement**:
- Top-down: PFC Imagine-Final → unconscious (user's intuition)
- Bottom-up: unconscious Anchor-Schema requirement → PFC must engage
- PFC is **not always in control** — the unconscious can pull PFC in

**This is NOT "another mechanism" — it is the reverse flow of the same mechanism.**

#### §2.8.3 — Predictive Processing (Open Frontier)

Framework acknowledges (Feeling-Research §9, Feel-Development §2.5):
> "Predictive processing (Friston, Seth) offers a framework. Interoceptive inference
> model (Seth 2013). Relationship to framework not fully worked out."

Predictive processing proposes: the brain is a **prediction machine** that generates
expectations and updates from prediction errors. If true, there may be deeper
unification:
- "Feeling" = PFC observing prediction error signals
- "Body-first" = body interoception happens upstream of prediction
- "Imagine-final" = forward model predictions
- "Anchor-schema" = high-level priors

**Theme A's stance on PP**: Accept as a promising direction but do not commit. Theme
A architecture is **compatible** with predictive processing but does not require it.

**→ Answer Part 3**: No, there is no secret "third mechanism." Framework has:
- ONE observation interface (PFC observes integrated unconscious processing)
- BIDIRECTIONAL flow (top-down attention + bottom-up requirement)
- MANY sources feeding into integration (10 channels in Feeling-Sources)
- POTENTIAL deeper unification via predictive processing (not yet committed)
