## §2 PFC as Observer (Not Executor)

[Phase 4 fill]

### §2.1 The role
PFC has READ ACCESS to integrated upstream signals. Does NOT execute
body processes, schemas, predictions, or simulations. Those run
elsewhere.

### §2.2 What PFC does
- Observe (read integrated signals)
- Label (via chunks)
- Integrate (combine multiple channel reads)
- Plan (strategic reasoning)
- Report (verbalize if chunks support)

### §2.3 What PFC doesn't do
- Execute interoception (body does)
- Run schemas (cortex does)
- Generate feelings (unconscious processing does)
- Directly access raw body (integration happens upstream)

### §2.4 7-layer structure refined
- Layer 1-2: upstream integration (PFC no direct access)
- Layer 3-4: PFC's read interface (genuine "felt sense")
- Layer 5-7: chunk-based labeling (error-prone)

This partially resolves Q3 (Logic-Feeling flow):
- NOT Model A (PFC directly senses raw body)
- NOT Model B (PFC pure labels from outside)
- IS: PFC reads integrated middle-ground signals, labels via chunks

### §2.5 Alignments
- Predictive Processing (PFC tracks errors not generates)
- Damasio "feeling of what happens"
- Gendlin felt sense
- Buddhist mindfulness (consciousness as observer)

## §4 Multi-Axis Development Model

[Phase 4 fill — this is the CORE of this file]

### §4.1 Why multi-axis, not single linear stages

Single stages too simplistic. Reality: individuals have **profile
vectors** — deep on some channels, shallow on others, varying speed,
integration, coupling.

### §4.2 The 5 axes

| Axis | Name | Range | Mechanism |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Depth per channel | Shallow ↔ Deep | Chunk library richness within single channel |
| 2 | Breadth across channels | Narrow ↔ Broad | Number of channels accessible |
| 3 | Speed / Automaticity | Effortful ↔ Automatic | Practice converts effortful to automatic |
| 4 | Integration capacity | Single ↔ Multi-channel | Simultaneous observation capability |
| 5 | Action coupling | Disconnected ↔ Tight | Observation → response translation |

Possible 6th axis: Meta-awareness (awareness of awareness)

### §4.3 Profile vectors — real-world examples

- **Olympic Athlete**: deep body, shallow emotional, fast body
  reactions, single-channel focus, tight body-action coupling
- **Long-term Meditator**: deep body + emotional + meta, broad,
  variable speed, high integration, moderate coupling
- **Successful Therapist**: deep social + emotional, broad, fast
  social reading, high integration, tight helping coupling
- **Pure Intellectual**: deep cognitive/metacognitive, often shallow
  body/emotional, fast cognitive, variable integration
- **Alexithymic**: shallow Axis 1 across, particularly emotional
- **Balanced Developer**: moderate across many channels (natural
  scaffolded child)
- **Trauma Survivor**: distorted profile, suppression patterns

### §4.4 Specialization vs Balance

Most experts spike 1-2 channels. Time/practice constraints make
balance at extreme depth impossible. Society needs both.

### §4.5 Blindspot generation

Deep on one channel → systematic blindspots on others. Not pathology,
byproduct of resource allocation. Examples:
- Athlete who can't read social cues
- Therapist disconnected from own body
- Intellectual without emotional access
- CEO who ignores fatigue

### §4.6 Cross-channel scaffolding

Similar channels transfer. "Biết buồn đái rồi → dễ biết buồn ỉa hơn".
But transfer imperfect — depth in X doesn't automatically transfer
to Y.

## §5 Math Formula Analogy

[Phase 4 fill]

### §5.1 The analogy
Chunks for feeling detection are like math formulas:
- Đếm số (Stage 0)
- Cộng trừ (Stage 1)
- Nhân chia (Stage 2)
- Đại số (Stage 3)
- Sin-cos / Calculus (specialization branches)
- Quantum (deep specialization)

### §5.2 Shared properties
- Cumulative: foundation required
- Branching: multiple specialization paths
- Specialization possible without mastering all
- Most people stop at intermediate
- Some go very deep in specific domains
- Cross-branch knowledge possible but not automatic
- Different chains per person

### §5.3 Applied to feeling chunks
- Distress/comfort → discriminate types → verbal labels → complex
  emotions → Resonance ToM → metacognitive feeling → existential
- Similar branching: body deep vs social deep vs cognitive deep
- Each feeling domain = specialization tree

### §5.4 Implications
- Feeling literacy can be TAUGHT (like math)
- Foundation must be built first (can't skip stages)
- Specialization possible (don't need everything)
- Most people stop at functional level
- Expert literacy is possible with training
