**Tier 2** ([Feel-Development.md](../Feel-Development.md) §1 OUTLINE, 2026-04-14):

> "ALL are PFC observing **unconscious processing**, whether: body (interoception), schemas (pattern matching), predictions (interoceptive + exteroceptive forecasts), memory retrieval, inference, simulator (Resonance output), integration (diffuse multi-source states)."

**Tension resolved**: Tier 1 attempted to list all sources inside "body systems" — which required a stretch. Tier 2 renames the category: "unconscious processing" is the parent; body-base is a subset.

### §2.2 — The Body-First Invariant (preserved unchanged)

**This part is stable across both Tier 1 and Tier 2**: Body → Feeling → PFC. The order is NEVER reversed.

**Four converging lines of evidence from Feeling-Accuracy.md §1:**

1. **Libet-style research** 🟢 (interpretation requires caveat): Unconscious brain activity precedes conscious decision by 300–500ms. PFC "decides" AFTER the body has already prepared the action.

2. **Damasio VMPFC patients** 🟢: Phineas Gage + modern cases. VMPFC damage → logic, memory, and language INTACT; feeling-based decision-making LOST → paralysis. Iowa Gambling Task: normal subjects develop anticipatory skin conductance response BEFORE consciously knowing which deck is bad. VMPFC patients: no anticipatory skin conductance response, cannot learn.

3. **Interoception** 🟢 (Craig 2002, 2013): Afferent pathways body → brain are continuous. The insula integrates body state BEFORE cortical processing. PFC receives an ALREADY-INTEGRATED signal. **PFC does not access raw body signals directly.**

4. **Gendlin Felt Sense** 🟢: 50+ years of clinical observation. "Felt Sense = body knowledge, pre-verbal. Cannot be generated by thinking. Must be ACCESSED, not created."

**Implications** (from Feeling-Accuracy.md §1):
> "A. 'Think yourself into feeling' = **impossible**. You can trigger a schema that triggers a body state (indirect). But you cannot directly will a feeling into existence.
>
> B. PFC can INHIBIT feeling expression, not generate the source. Suppression works temporarily, but the source feeling continues at the body level.
>
> C. Awareness matters: The body computes whether PFC notices or not. PFC attention = whether the feeling becomes explicit. Not attending ≠ not happening."

**→ INVARIANT**: Regardless of which definition (Tier 1 or Tier 2), **the body computes first, feeling emerges, PFC observes after**. Theme A preserves this fully.

### §2.3 — Why "body-base" alone is too narrow (evidence for Tier 2)

The Feel-Development outline identifies six types of feeling that do not fit purely body-base:

- But IS a real feeling PFC observes

- Example: "I feel confident about this argument" (E79)
- Source: schema fit + prediction coherence
- The body participates (mild arousal), but the primary source is cognitive schemas

**Example 3 — Social intuition:**
- Example: "I feel like this person can't be trusted" (E88)
- Source: Resonance output (simulator running other-state using own chunks)
- NOT pure body interoception — **the simulator is running another person's state**

**Example 4 — Aesthetic feeling:**
- Example: "Artist notices when the painting 'works'" (E95)
- Source: compiled aesthetic chunks firing
- Diffuse body response, but the core is pattern-match at the cognitive level

**Example 5 — Predictive interoception:**
- Example: "Urge to urinate before the actual need arrives" (E80)
- Source: **predictive processing** — the body anticipates a future state, not the current state
- Craig 2009 "How do you feel — now?" extended to predictive interoception (Seth 2013)

**Example 6 — Gendlin Felt Sense:**
- Pre-verbal body wisdom
- Source: integrated multi-channel compilation
- Body is the source — but **what is integrated** includes schemas, memory, prediction, not just raw interoception

**Pattern across all six**: All are real feelings PFC observes. None fit a clean "body-base" definition. All involve **unconscious processing beyond raw body signals** — schemas, predictions, simulation, memory retrieval.

**Framework's own acknowledgment** (Feeling-Research.md §9 Research limits):

### §2.5 — PFC's Role: Observer, Not Creator

Feel-Development.md §2 explicitly lists what PFC does and does not do:

**What PFC DOES:**
- **Observe** (read integrated upstream signals)
- **Label** (via chunks — error-prone at Feel-Labeling–Feel-Explanation)
- **Integrate** (combine multiple channel reads)
- **Plan** (strategic reasoning using chunks)
- **Report** (verbalize if chunks support)
- **Choose** between competing signals (executive function)
- **Direct attention** (top-down bias of what to observe)

**What PFC DOESN'T do:**
- **Execute interoception** (the body does)
- **Run schemas** (the cortex runs them automatically)
- **Generate feelings** (unconscious processing does)
- **Directly access raw body signals** (integration happens upstream)

**Imagine-Final.md §1 adds three specific PFC functions:**
1. **Bring to consciousness**: name the unconscious Imagine-Final
2. **Simulate domain**: check feasibility, domain accuracy
3. **Choose**: executive choice between competing Imagine-Finals

**PFC = REACTIVE, not proactive** (Imagine-Final §1):
> "PFC does NOT self-initiate → BODY CALLS PFC when needed. Body MET → PFC does not engage (autopilot). Body UNMET → dissonance signal → PFC ACTIVATES."

**~95/5 rule** (Anchor-Schema §1):
- ~95% of life: compiled schemas auto-fire → auto-anchor → PFC does NOT participate
- ~5% of decisions: compiled schemas insufficient → PFC engages (new situations, conflicts, long-term decisions)

**→ Answer to sub-question 1**: PFC does "observe the unconscious then choose" — **the framework explicitly confirms this**. But PFC does more than just observe and choose:
- Observe (Feel-Consciousification–Feel-Observation integration)
- Label via chunks (Feel-Labeling, error-prone)
- Explain via narrative (Feel-Explanation, confabulation risk)
- Choose between competing signals (executive)
- Direct attention (top-down bias)

### §2.6 — "Directing the unconscious to compute or hold for chaining": Checking the Hypothesis

The question is: **Is there a top-down "query mechanism" where PFC directs the unconscious to continue computing, or to hold a result for chaining?**

**Framework's position** (partial support, needs refinement):

**Direct evidence in the framework:**
- Imagine-Final.md §1 Layer 2: PFC performs deliberate operations — bring to consciousness, simulate domain, choose
- Imagine-Final.md §1.5 Phase 4 RELOAD: "body triggers PFC to re-engage with the SAVED Imagine-Final" — top-down reload
- Anchor-Schema.md §1 step ③: "PFC SCANS + IMAGINES: searches for a new anchor" — PFC actively generates candidates when the compiled anchor is insufficient

**Indirect support from research:**
- **Global Workspace Theory** (Baars 1988) 🟢: PFC broadcast → whole-brain sync. This IS a top-down mechanism.
- **Predictive processing** (Clark 2013, Friston): PFC sets high-level priors → propagated down to sensory cortex. Top-down attention biases what the unconscious processes.
- **Miller and Cohen 2001** 🟢: PFC maintains goal representations → biases processing across the brain. Not just passive observation.

**BUT**: The framework has not explicitly modeled **"PFC query → wait for response → chain"** as a distinct mechanism.

**Closest framework concept — "Attention allocation"** (Feeling.md §2.5 Feel-Observation):
> "Feel-Observation requires PFC allocation (attention budget)."

**And Imagine-Final §1.5 BACKGROUND phase:**
> "Forcing active thought is LESS EFFECTIVE than letting it absorb naturally." Most of the Imagine-Final's evolution happens IN PHASE 3 (background) — NOT during active PFC work.

**Key insight**: The framework says PFC can **allocate attention** (top-down) but **chain formation happens mostly in the background** (not during active PFC work). The hypothesis is **partially correct**:

- **YES**: PFC can direct attention, trigger reload, choose which simulation to run
- **NO** (or partial): PFC doesn't "query and wait" — instead, PFC sets attention, releases control to the unconscious, receives a refined result upon reload

**Refined picture**: The framework architecture is:
1. PFC sets attention / anchor / question
2. PFC **releases** to unconscious processing (Background Phase)
3. Unconscious consolidates, spreads activation, refines chunks via REM + default mode network
4. When the body triggers (dissonance) or PFC queries (reload), PFC pulls the result back
5. If unfinished, the loop repeats

**This is close to "directing the unconscious to compute or hold"** but with a nuance: PFC doesn't stay active waiting. It disengages and reloads later.

**→ Theme A confirms the hypothesis with refinement**: A top-down query exists (attention allocation + anchor setting). Chain formation itself is NOT active PFC work — it is background processing. PFC reloads to see progress. The intuition captures the direction-setting aspect correctly but may overstate how "active" the waiting period is.

### §2.7 — Spectrum: Clear Signals → Fuzzy Imagine Layer

The original question describes a spectrum: "from PFC detecting basic body-base signals (hunger, clear pain, clear joy, ...) to PFC detecting through a dim, fuzzy imagine layer in the unconscious."

**Framework's decomposition of this spectrum:**

#### §2.7.1 — Feel-Consciousification Clarity Dimension (from Feeling.md §2)

The 7-layer structure applies to ALL feeling sources, not just body interoception:

| Layer | Clarity | Clear signal example | Fuzzy signal example |
|---|---|---|---|
| Feel-RawSignals | 100% body truth | Gastric signals | Schema partial fire |
| Feel-Integration | 95% | Insula integration | Multi-schema integration |
| Feel-Consciousification | 90% | Clear hunger | "There's something..." |
| Feel-Observation | 85% | "I'm hungry" | "I'm feeling something" |
| Feel-Location | 70–90% | "Hunger in my stomach" | "In my chest but dim" |
| Feel-Labeling | 40–80% | "Hungry" | "Uneasy" |
| Feel-Explanation | 20–70% | "Because I haven't eaten" | "Because... I don't know" |

**Clear signals (hunger, clear pain)** reliably reach Feel-Location–Feel-Labeling. Fuzzy signals remain at Feel-Consciousification–Feel-Observation.

**This is NOT two different mechanisms** — it is the **same observation interface** applied to signals of different strength and source.

#### §2.7.2 — Source Dimension

From Feeling-Sources.md §0–§10, ten channels produce feelings. They differ in:

- **Source strength**: L1 physiological (high, clear) vs Imagine-Final preview (low, fuzzy)
- **Temporal stability**: L1 (stable over time) vs cognitive prediction (flickering)
- **Compilation depth**: meaning (evaluative, deep compile) vs novel cross-domain (no compile yet)

**Clear hunger:**
- Source: L1 interoception (gastric, ghrelin signals)
- Integration: clear — single-source dominant
- Feel-Consciousification fidelity: high
- PFC labels easily: "hungry"

**Fuzzy Imagine-Final preview:**
- Source: simulation of a future state
- Integration: weak — the preview is a diluted version of the "real" body response
- Feel-Consciousification fidelity: lower
- PFC labels with uncertainty: "I feel like this plan will work... maybe?"

**Same mechanism, different signal strength.** The framework handles both through the same PFC observation interface.

#### §2.7.3 — Why "Fuzzy" Feels Different (Feel-Consciousification Felt Sense)

**Gendlin's Felt Sense** (Feeling.md §2.4):
> "Feel-Consciousification is the point where the body has finished computing — but PFC has not yet labeled anything. 'Something's there,' 'something feels off,' 'I don't know but I feel...' = the TRUTH is already present. PFC just doesn't have words for it yet."

**Why some feelings feel "fuzzy":**
1. **Source is upstream integration** (not a single raw signal) → no single word maps to it
2. **Multiple channels firing weakly** → compression is difficult
3. **PFC chunks insufficient** → no learned label exists for this specific pattern
4. **Novel combination** → schemas don't have a pre-compiled label

**Framework position** (from Feel-Development §2.4):
> "The Logic-Feeling flow is partially resolved: NOT Model A (PFC directly senses raw body), NOT Model B (PFC purely labels from outside), IS: **PFC reads integrated middle-ground signals, labels via chunks.**"

**→ Fuzzy ≠ another mechanism.** Fuzzy = the same PFC observation interface seeing a signal that:
- Is integrated (Feel-Integration–Feel-Consciousification) but
- Has no compiled Feel-Labeling label yet

**Training (meditation, Gendlin Focusing)** = learning to trust Feel-Consciousification–Feel-Observation without forcing premature Feel-Labeling labels.

### §2.8 — "Or is there another processing mechanism?" — Sub-question 3

The question: Is there another processing mechanism beyond "PFC observes the unconscious + chooses + requests"?

**Framework's answer**: **Possibly, but the framework commits to a unified model** with bidirectional flow.

#### §2.8.1 — No separate "imagine layer" mechanism

The framework does NOT treat "imagine layer feelings" as a separate mechanism:
- Imagine-Final previews = body simulation firing the same pathways as real experience (Schema-Operations §8.5, cited in Feeling.md)
- Mirror "copy" = own body firing a weaker version of another's state (Gallese embodied simulation)
- Schema-triggered = body fires as if real input (Feeling-Sources §7)

**Common pattern**: The body fires the same pathways at different intensities, from different triggers. PFC observes the integrated result. ONE mechanism, many inputs.

#### §2.8.2 — Bidirectional flow is the architectural complement

Anchor-Schema.md §0 adds the **bottom-up complement:**
- Top-down: PFC Imagine-Final → unconscious (the original intuition)
- Bottom-up: Unconscious Anchor-Schema requirement → PFC must engage
- PFC is **not always in control** — the unconscious can pull PFC in

**This is NOT "another mechanism" — it is the reverse flow of the same mechanism.**

#### §2.8.3 — Predictive Processing (open frontier)

The framework acknowledges (Feeling-Research §9, Feel-Development §2.5):
> "Predictive processing (Friston, Seth) offers a framework. Interoceptive inference model (Seth 2013). Relationship to the framework not fully worked out."

Predictive processing proposes: the brain is a **prediction machine** that generates expectations and updates from prediction errors. If true, this may enable a deeper unification:
- "Feeling" = PFC observing prediction error signals
- "Body-first" = body interoception happens upstream of prediction
- "Imagine-Final" = forward model predictions
- "Anchor-Schema" = high-level priors

**Theme A's stance on predictive processing**: Accept as a promising direction but do not commit. Theme A architecture is **compatible** with predictive processing but does not require it.

**→ Answer to sub-question 3**: No, there is no secret "third mechanism." The framework has:
- ONE observation interface (PFC observes integrated unconscious processing)
- BIDIRECTIONAL flow (top-down attention + bottom-up requirement)
- MANY sources feeding into integration (10 channels in Feeling-Sources)
- POTENTIAL deeper unification via predictive processing (not committed yet)

**The framework is NOT contradicting itself — it is refining.**
