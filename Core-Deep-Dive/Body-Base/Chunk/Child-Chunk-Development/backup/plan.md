## §0 — North Star

### §0.1 — Goal (concise)

Formalize **how biological substrate compiles raw sensory input into chunks** during 0-24mo developmental arc, organized by **body-input systems** (what baby needs to connect, read-understand, control) and validated through **observable events** from which chunks state can be inferred.

### §0.2 — Why this folder matters

1. **Foundation layer for all downstream work**: Chunks must form before F3 Language can install on top, before F4 Internal Processing can transform them, before 99-Master-Synthesis can commit architectural claims about H1 (chunk substrate). F1 is prerequisite.

2. **Cleanest test case**: Pre-verbal infant 0-24mo has **no language confound** (verbal chunks don't exist yet for most of this window), **clear developmental gradient** (chunks visibly accumulating week-by-week), and **abundant observable events** already documented in `Feel-Example-Draft.md` §1-§3 (30+ cases).

3. **Validation for H10 P2 bottleneck**: Body-Feedback-Draft (N+3) identified H10 P2 "Chunks Base Adequacy" as the keystone developmental bottleneck — **how** chunks accumulate from zero at birth to adequate threshold is F1's central mechanism question.

4. **Answers user's accumulated academic questions**: receptive-productive gap mechanism, infant name recognition timing (Mandel 4.5mo), first chunk minimum experience, multi-modal binding mechanism, pre-verbal chunks vs verbal chunks distinction.

5. **Framework contribution opportunity**: PFC-Inference Ladder formalization of user's "đơ mặt" hypothesis — novel reframe of PFC effectiveness as chunks-driven event property, not PFC maturation timeline.

### §0.3 — Methodological commitment: event-inference

**What we DO claim** (weak but defensible):
- "Event X is observable at age Y in baby population"
- "Event X plausibly requires chunks A, B, C to have compiled"
- "Therefore by age Y, chunks A, B, C must have accumulated"
- "This places F1 substrate chunk compile mechanism within observable inference"

**What we DO NOT claim** (would be overclaim):
- "PFC activates at age X" — we cannot directly measure PFC activation in infants without invasive scanning
- "PFC matures at age X" — maturation is multi-dimensional (myelination vs pruning vs integration) and not a single threshold
- "PFC controls behavior X" — direct causal claims require lesion evidence we don't have for developing PFC

**Epistemological frame**: We are **inferring chunks state from observable behavior**, not measuring PFC maturation. User's insight (2026-04-15):

> "Câu hỏi không phải là ở giai đoạn nào có PFC. Mà chúng ta chỉ cần liệt kê các event mà có khả năng có PFC. như vậy đúng ko nhỉ. vì chúng ta không đo lường được PFC thực sự."

This is correct epistemology. F1 drill adopts this commitment throughout — every PFC-involvement claim carries a plausibility qualifier (🟡), every observable claim is either 🟢 empirically supported or 🟡 framework inference.

## §0.6 — Convergence Points (7 Nút Thắt F1 phải hội tụ)

> F1 drill has many files and examples, but converges on **7 architectural questions** that must be resolved (verdict or pending-research) by end of F1. These are the load-bearing claims F1 commits to 99-Master-Synthesis.

### Nút Thắt 1 — Proto-chunk verdict: discrete vs gradient

**Question**: Does "proto-chunk" exist as intermediate stage between raw firing and stable compiled chunk, or is chunk compilation a discrete threshold event?

**Why load-bearing**: H1 (chunk substrate) architecture differs:
- **Gradient view**: substrate is continuum from weak patterns → stable chunks; proto-chunks are meaningful intermediate states
- **Discrete view**: chunk exists or not; pre-chunk activity is just noise

**Candidate answer from LTP mechanism**: Early-LTP (minutes-hours, protein-synthesis-independent) vs Late-LTP (hours-days, protein-synthesis-dependent) suggests **gradient biological reality**. Proto-chunk = early-LTP state not yet consolidated to late-LTP stable chunk.

**Drill home**: `04-Sensory-Reception-Arc.md` §3 (auditory phoneme discrimination gradient) + `07-Interoceptive-Arc.md` §4 (bladder signal detection emergence over weeks) + `09-F1-Synthesis.md` final verdict.

### Nút Thắt 2 — P2 Chunks Base Adequacy compile mechanism ⭐ KEYSTONE

**Question**: HOW do chunks accumulate from zero at birth to the "adequate threshold" required for body reward signal to fire (H10 P2 precondition from Body-Feedback-Draft N+3)?

**Why load-bearing**: This is THE keystone link between Body-Feedback-Draft (N+3 drilled, H10) and F1 (N+4 drilling). Body-feedback substrate (L1-L2 raw signals) **invariant from birth** — same ghrelin, same nociceptor, same cortisol. But **Feeling layer resolution** (L3-L7 labeled observations) varies dramatically newborn → adult. The variance is P2 Chunks Base: rich chunks → fine feeling resolution; minimal chunks → crude body signal only.

If P2 mechanism not resolved, entire H10 developmental bootstrap has no substrate-level grounding.

**Mechanism candidates**:
- Repetition compile (LTP) — chunks form from repeated sensor firing (hundreds of repetitions per basic chunk)
- Emotional peak compile — flashbulb chunks (Brown & Kulik 1977) from high-valence events (Moro scare, feeding reward)
- Multi-modal binding — cross-sensor co-activation strengthens binding
- Sleep consolidation — infant sleeps 16+ hrs/day, most chunk consolidation happens offline

**Drill home**: `07-Interoceptive-Arc.md` §4-§5 (body state chunks accumulation — most visible bottleneck for buồn đái case) + `09-F1-Synthesis.md` §2 (P2 bottleneck formalization + link to H10).

### Nút Thắt 3 — Multi-modal binding at birth mechanism

**Question**: HOW does newborn bind visual + auditory + somatic + emotional inputs into a **single unified chunk** when there is no verbal label and PFC coordination capacity is still compiling?

**Key case**: Social smile 6-8 weeks (Feel-Example E12) = first documented multi-modal binding (face + voice + body calm → smile response). How does the binding occur without a "coordinator"?

**Candidate mechanisms**:
- **Temporal co-occurrence + Hebbian binding** — if visual fires with auditory fires with somatic within 100-500ms window → synaptic binding strengthens
- **Multisensory neurons** — superior colliculus + STS (superior temporal sulcus) have neurons responding to multiple modalities (Meredith & Stein 1983, Stein & Meredith 1993)
- **Default Mode Network early scaffolding** — Doria 2010 shows frontoparietal + DMN networks formed by term birth, providing substrate architecture for cross-modal integration
- **Amygdala emotional tag as binding cue** — emotional co-activation (warmth + face + voice = comfort) binds via amygdala-mediated association

**Drill home**: `04-Sensory-Reception-Arc.md` §6 (multi-modal binding mechanism) + `06-Social-Recognition-Arc.md` §2 (E12 social smile decomposition).

### Nút Thắt 5 — Receptive-Productive Gap mechanism (H11)

**Question**: WHY does comprehension consistently form 6-12 months before production capability? Name recognition 4.5mo (Mandel Jusczyk & Pisoni 1995) vs first verbal label "đói" 18mo (E21) = 13.5 month gap.

**Old answer (rejected)**: "PFC maturation delay" — incompatible with PFC hardware reframe.

**New mechanism candidate**: **Asymmetric chunks accumulation requirement**:
- **Comprehension chunks**: auditory pattern chunk + referent chunk + meaning binding chunk (3 chunk types)
- **Production chunks**: comprehension chunks + motor articulation chunks + social feedback loop chunks + grammar scaffolding chunks (6+ chunk types, 2x the accumulation requirement)
- Gap = production requires 2x chunks → 2x accumulation time → 6-12 months delay

**Alternative hypotheses to reject**:
- Motor development lag alone (falsified: gesture production at 14mo E25 precedes verbal production at 18mo E21, same motor system shows production 4mo earlier if using gesture)
- Cognitive capacity limit (falsified: comprehension IS cognitively complex, and it happens first)
- Pure developmental sequence without mechanism (circular, not explanatory)

**Drill home**: `09-Receptive-Productive-Gap.md` §3 mechanism decomposition + §4 H11 formalization with falsifiable predictions.

### Nút Thắt 6 — Verbal label as "handle" mechanism

**Question**: When toddler says "đói" at 18mo (E21), chunk "hunger state" already existed from E1 at 0-3mo. What does verbal label **add**? How does label "attach" to pre-existing chunk?

**Framework claim** (from Feel-Example-Draft §3 line 1403-1408): "Language creates HANDLES for chunks." But mechanism undefined.

**Candidate mechanisms**:
- **Label = 5th modality add-on** (multi-modal binding extended) — verbal input joins existing visual/auditory/somatic/emotional modalities binding into unified chunk
- **Label = symbolic compression token** (Miller 1956 chunking efficiency) — verbal label compresses multi-modal chunk into PFC-holdable unit
- **Label = retrieval path** (not constitutive) — label is one of multiple access paths, pre-existing chunk doesn't become "more real" with label

**Load-bearing for H1**: If label is constitutive ("no chunk without verbal"), H1 chunk substrate claim weakens (chunks dependent on language scaffolding). If label is modality add-on, H1 strengthens (chunks exist independently, verbal enhances retrieval/compression/communication).

**Evidence from pre-verbal chunks**: Infants CLEARLY have chunks before verbal (face preference, name recognition, object permanence, etc.). So label is NOT constitutive. But exact mechanism of attachment still needs drill.

**Drill home**: `08-Verbal-Production-Arc.md` §4 (verbal emergence mechanism) + `09-Receptive-Productive-Gap.md` bridge to F3 Language folder.

### Nút Thắt 7 — Cortisol tagging critical period in infancy

**Question**: HOW does cortisol context during chunk compile create a "tag" (H10 P5 precondition) that persists decades into adulthood, affecting lifelong reward accessibility?

**Why load-bearing**: H10 Prediction #8 (Body-Feedback-Draft 04-Integration §9.3) claims childhood threat-learned content elicits measurable physiological discomfort during adult retrieval. This is a bold claim requiring mechanism at chunk formation level. Infancy 0-12mo is P5 critical period.

**Mechanism candidate**:
- Chunks compile with concurrent hippocampal-amygdala-cortisol state
- Encoding captures state as metadata (neurochemical tag)
- Retrieval activates chunk content AND associated tag via hippocampal-amygdala co-activation
- Tag persistence = memory trace includes association to affective state at compile time

**Framework implication**: Infant caregiver environment (threat vs safe curious learning) shapes lifelong reward accessibility via P5 tagging. Critical window 0-12mo when most basic chunks forming.

**Drill home**: `07-Interoceptive-Arc.md` §5 (body-state chunks form under caregiver emotional context) + `09-F1-Synthesis.md` §3 (P5 mechanism with developmental implications).

## §0.7 — PFC-Inference Ladder (Framework Contribution) ⭐

> **Framework contribution from F1 drill**: Formalize user's "đơ mặt" insight (this session 2026-04-15) into a 5-level event classifier that provides epistemologically honest inference from observable events to chunks state, **without over-claiming PFC developmental timeline**.
>
> **Critical framing distinction**: This is a **classifier for events by plausible chunks requirement**, NOT a "PFC developmental stages" timeline. A given age can have events at multiple ladder levels simultaneously. Same event can appear at multiple ladders over time as chunks accumulate.

### §0.7.1 — Methodological foundation

**Why this ladder matters**:

1. Literature typically treats PFC involvement as binary (offline vs capable), missing intermediate stages
2. Parents/observers actually see intermediate behaviors (freeze, puzzled face, uncoordinated response, crude communication) — these need a framework
3. PFC cannot be measured directly in infants (no invasive scanning) — inference from events is the only available method
4. User's insight: **observable face/body reactions give empirical handles on PFC pattern-match firing, even when action capability absent**

**What the ladder does**:
- Provides 5 categorical levels for classifying observable events
- Each level has clear behavioral markers (empirical grip)
- Each level has explicit plausible chunks requirement (inference content)
- Each level preserves plausibility qualifier 🟡 (no overclaim)

**What the ladder does NOT do**:
- Claim "at age X, baby is at ladder level Y" — would be overclaim
- Assume uniform progression across body-inputs — different systems climb at different rates
- Specify exact neural substrate — stays at functional/observable level

### §0.7.2 — The 5 Levels

#### **L0 — Reflex-only events**

**Definition**: Event executed by innate brainstem/spinal circuits. No learned chunks required. No PFC pattern-match involvement.

**Behavioral markers**:
- Triggered by specific stimulus (no learning history required)
- Stereotyped response (always same)
- Present at birth or within hours
- Preserved even when cortex compromised

**Example events**:
- First cry at birth (brainstem respiratory reflex — lung inflation)
- Moro startle (E6) — acoustic → full-body extension
- Palmar grasp (E7) — tactile palm → finger closure
- Rooting + sucking (E8) — cheek touch → head turn + suck
- Patellar reflex, Babinski sign

**Chunks required**: None learned. Innate hardwired circuits only.

**PFC plausibility**: 🟢 No PFC involvement inferable. These events happen in anencephalic infants (born without cortex).

#### **L1 — Post-hoc response events**

**Definition**: Event where body signal fires AFTER triggering event, producing crude reactive response. Chunks compiling via repetition but not yet stable enough for pre-event detection.

**Behavioral markers**:
- Response happens AFTER stimulus event, not pre-emptively
- Response is crude (cry, distress) not specific
- Baby seems unaware of antecedent pattern
- Response intensity depends on stimulus intensity, not context

**Example events**:
- Newborn đái → wet diaper → discomfort → khóc (post-hoc interoceptive)
- Newborn pain (E2 vaccination initial touch) → cry (post-hoc nociceptive)
- Newborn hunger (E1) → escalating cry (post-hoc visceral)
- Newborn cold/hot discomfort (E4) → fuss (post-hoc thermal)

**Chunks required**: Raw interoceptive/sensory chunks (L1-L2 body-feedback in Body-Feedback-Draft layer model). Not yet associative/predictive chunks. Repetition compile happening but not yet functionally consolidated.

**PFC plausibility**: 🟡 PFC hardware active (per reframe) but no pre-event pattern-match firing yet. PFC may observe post-hoc but doesn't contribute to response (cry is L1-L2 body-feedback driven).

#### **L2 — Pattern-match activation events** ⭐ USER'S "ĐƠ MẶT" INSIGHT

**Definition**: Event where chunks compiled enough to fire on antecedent signal. PFC pattern-match "lights up" when familiar pattern detected, BUT no action chunks available. Observable as behavioral freeze, facial expression change, anticipatory tension, prolonged looking, or pre-emptive distress.

**Critical distinction**: L2 is NOT "PFC trying to act and failing". L2 IS "PFC pattern-match fires on pattern recognition, no action intention involved". The observable reaction is body-level manifestation of **recognition without execution**, not "frustration at inability".

**Behavioral markers**:
- Reaction happens BEFORE triggering event completes (pre-emptive)
- Reaction is SIGNAL of recognition (facial freeze, looking duration change, anticipatory tension) not an ACTION
- Similar events in past produced full consequences (chunks compiled from those)
- Baby cannot yet avoid, prevent, or communicate about the pattern

**Example events** (⭐ PRIMARY F1 empirical handles):

| Marker | Age | System | Description |
|---|---|---|---|
| **"Đơ mặt" buồn đái** | 12-18mo | Interoceptive | User's hypothesis — bladder signal fires, chunk "signal → wet → discomfort" activates, facial freeze shows PFC engagement, still wets |
| **Vaccination pre-cry** | 6-12mo | Visual+emotional | Baby cries upon seeing doctor/clinic before touching happens (Pierce & Rodrigues 1996) — chunks compiled from prior visit, pattern match fires |
| **Stranger anxiety face progression** | 7-9mo | Social | Baby sees stranger → face transitions curious → puzzled → distressed over 5-10 sec (Sroufe 1977). Each phase = PFC pattern-match attempts. Duration = processing time. |
| **Still face paradigm Phase 3** | 6mo+ | Social | Mother freezes face → baby attempts re-engagement → **puzzled freeze** (L2 marker) → distress withdrawal (Tronick 1978). Expectation chunk violated. |
| **Baillargeon VoE longer looking** | 4-6mo | Visual+cognitive | Impossible physical event → baby looks longer (sustained attention = PFC engagement measure). Object permanence chunk violated. |
| **Peek-a-boo anticipation tension** | 10-14mo | Social+temporal | Before reveal, baby face shows tension (positive valence L2). Prediction chunk fires before event. |
| **Mother recognition face** (E15) | 5mo | Multi-modal | Baby face lights up seeing mother — recognition chunks fire (face + voice + smell + warmth bind) |
| **Vaccination anticipation (room alone)** | 12-18mo | Multi-modal | Baby cries upon ENTERING room, before seeing doctor. Environment chunks fire from entire clinic context. |

**Chunks required**: Associative chunks (antecedent → consequence) compiled from prior experience. Stable enough to fire on antecedent alone. Action chunks NOT required (L2 has no execution component).

**PFC plausibility**: 🟡🟢 Strong inference PFC pattern-match firing. Observable behavioral reaction implies internal chunk activation. This is the "sweet spot" ladder level — empirically well-supported, frequent in infant population, visible to caregivers, measurable in controlled paradigms (VoE looking time, still face phase transitions).

**Why L2 is load-bearing for F1**: L2 events are the **empirical backbone** for PFC hardware reframe. If PFC were offline, L2 behaviors impossible (no pattern-match firing → no freeze/anticipation/looking prolongation). L2 being observable = falsification of old "PFC offline" view.

#### **L3 — Crude response events**

**Definition**: Pattern-match fires + partial action chunks available. Response is uncoordinated, vague, unreliable — but present. Baby attempts action without coordination or specific communication.

**Behavioral markers**:
- Response has action component (not just freeze/anticipation)
- Action is uncoordinated, vague, or unreliable
- Multiple similar events → response quality varies
- Caregiver may or may not understand

**Example events**:
- Crude gesture toward object (proto-imperative, 8-10mo)
- Uncoordinated cry + squirm when buồn đái detected but no verbal
- Reaching toward moving object with wrong trajectory (early reaching 4mo)
- Cry contagion response (E9) — catches other's distress, responds vaguely
- Smile contagion (E13) — pattern-match mother's smile, returns crude smile
- Social referencing early (E19) — looks toward mother's face when uncertain, uses as vague safety signal

**Chunks required**: L2 chunks + partial motor chunks + partial social chunks. Not yet coordinated into stable action sequences.

**PFC plausibility**: 🟡 PFC pattern-match + partial action planning. More speculation than L2 since action involvement means more inference.

#### **L4 — Coordinated execution events**

**Definition**: Pattern-match fires + coordinated action chunks + (often) communicative component. Baby executes specific, reliable, goal-directed response.

**Behavioral markers**:
- Response is specific and reliable (repeatable)
- Response is goal-directed (clear intended outcome)
- Caregiver consistently understands
- May include verbal or gestural communication component

**Example events**:
- Joint attention (E18, 9-12mo) — coordinated gaze + gesture + caregiver attention integration
- Proto-declarative pointing (12-14mo) — points to share attention, not just to request
- Verbal "đói" (E21, 18mo) — interoceptive chunk + verbal label + request
- Verbal "đau chân" with pointing (E22, 20mo) — localization + verbal + body schema
- "Buồn đái" prediction + call mom (E23, 22mo) — full L4 execution for bladder system
- Comforting crying mother (E26, 18mo) — detects distress + applies comfort template + acts
- Imitation of household tasks (E29, 16mo) — delayed imitation + action sequence reproduction
- "Không" autonomy assertion (E31, 18-24mo) — self-agency chunk + verbal execution

**Chunks required**: Full chunks stack — associative + motor + social + (often) verbal. Coordinated into stable action sequences.

**PFC plausibility**: 🟡🟢 Strong PFC involvement. Coordinated execution requires PFC pattern-match + motor plan + (often) verbal production + monitoring. Most developmental milestones fall here.

### §0.7.3 — Ladder properties

**Property 1 — Ladder is event property, not age property**:
- A 9-month-old may have L0 events (still shows Moro if startled), L1 events (newborn-style crude hunger cry), L2 events (stranger anxiety face progression), L3 events (crude pointing), L4 events (joint attention) all concurrent.
- Classification depends on the specific event, not the baby's age.

**Property 2 — Same event climbs ladder over time**:
- Vaccination response example:
  - At 0-3mo: L0 (reflex cry to needle touch)
  - At 3-6mo: L1 (cry when needle penetrates, no earlier awareness)
  - At 6-9mo: L2 (cry when seeing needle approach)
  - At 9-12mo: L2-L3 (cry when doctor approaches with needle-like object)
  - At 12-18mo: L3 (attempts to avoid, squirm, protest)
  - At 18-24mo: L4 (verbal "không đi bác sĩ", planned resistance)

**Property 3 — Non-uniform ladder progression across body-inputs**:
- At a given age, different body-input systems are at different ladder levels simultaneously
- Example 10mo old: visual tracking at L4 (coordinated gaze), motor at L3 (crude reaching), bladder interoception at L0-L1 (no awareness), verbal production at L1-L2 (babbling forming, no words), social recognition at L2 (stranger anxiety)
- Progression rate = chunks accumulation rate per system, depends on exposure frequency + complexity

**Property 4 — Ladder classification is plausibility-qualified**:
- Every ladder classification carries 🟡 (framework inference) unless direct empirical evidence (🟢)
- L0 events have 🟢 (reflexes are well-established neurology)
- L2 events have 🟡-🟢 (behavioral markers empirical but PFC inference theoretical)
- L3-L4 events have 🟡 (more PFC inference → more uncertainty)

**Property 5 — Ladder gives falsifiable predictions**:
- Prediction 1: Any baby showing L2 events (anticipation faces, VoE looking time, etc.) has PFC pattern-match capacity active (falsifies old "PFC offline" view)
- Prediction 2: Vaccination anticipation earliest possible age correlates with object-context chunks compile rate (~6-9mo minimum per current data)
- Prediction 3: Non-uniform ladder progression means single-modality assessment unreliable as general developmental indicator
- Prediction 4: Ladder level achievable = chunks accumulation function, not PFC maturation function — correlates with exposure frequency not age alone

## §0.8 — Observable PFC pattern-match markers (empirical handles)

> F1 drill has 6 concrete observable markers for L2 "pattern-match activation" events. These are the empirical handles validating PFC hardware reframe. Each marker: age, body-input, observable signal, mechanism, research base, confidence.

### Marker 1: "Đơ mặt" buồn đái ⭐ USER ORIGINAL

- **Age**: ~12-18mo (individual variation, may appear earlier)
- **System**: Interoceptive (body state — bladder)
- **Observable signal**: Baby mid-play freezes face for 1-3 seconds, then still urinates, then discomfort/cry
- **Mechanism**: Interoceptive signal "bladder fullness" fires → associative chunk "signal → wet → discomfort" activates → PFC pattern-match → body-level facial freeze. No action chunks yet available → baby cannot avoid or communicate.
- **Research base**: 🟡 User's original empirical observation. Literature sparse on "đơ mặt" as specific marker, but consistent with infant interoceptive awareness emergence (Maister et al. 2017 on infant interoception). F1 drill can propose as novel empirical marker.
- **Confidence**: 🟡 Framework hypothesis, observable but not yet systematically studied
- **Drill home**: `07-Interoceptive-Arc.md` §4

### Marker 2: Vaccination pre-cry ⭐ STRONGEST EMPIRICAL

- **Age**: 6-18mo (progressively earlier as chunks compile across visits)
- **System**: Visual + emotional + multi-modal (clinic context chunks)
- **Observable signal**: Baby cries BEFORE stimulus (before needle touches). May cry upon seeing doctor, entering clinic room, or seeing white coat.
- **Mechanism**: Prior vaccination visit(s) compiled environmental chunks (clinic room + white coat + specific smells + pain association). Subsequent visit: environmental chunks fire → associative chunk "this context → pain" fires → anticipatory distress response.
- **Research base**: 🟢 Pierce & Rodrigues 1996 — infant distress anticipation at medical procedures documented. Cohen et al. infant immunization pain studies. Clinical knowledge — pediatricians regularly report this pattern.
- **Confidence**: 🟢 Strong empirical support, well-replicated, observable in any repeat vaccination visit
- **Drill home**: `04-Sensory-Reception-Arc.md` §5 (visual contextual chunks) + `07-Interoceptive-Arc.md` §6 (pain anticipation)

### Marker 3: Stranger anxiety face progression

- **Age**: 7-9mo onset (earlier or later with individual variation)
- **System**: Social recognition
- **Observable signal**: Baby sees stranger → facial expression transitions over 5-10 seconds: neutral → curious → puzzled/wary → distressed → crying/clinging. Parents can observe every phase.
- **Mechanism**: Visual chunk "face" matches CONSPEC template → "not mother" mismatch → "not familiar caregiver" mismatch → "unknown agent without safety context" distress chunk fires. Each phase = PFC pattern-match attempt against different template.
- **Research base**: 🟢 Sroufe 1977 stranger distress developmental studies. Kagan temperament research. Universal across cultures with onset timing variation.
- **Confidence**: 🟢 Strong empirical support, observable in any 8mo baby encounter with stranger
- **Drill home**: `06-Social-Recognition-Arc.md` §3

### Marker 4: Still face paradigm Phase 3 response

- **Age**: 6mo+ (response emerges after chunks for "mother responsive" compiled)
- **System**: Social + expectation
- **Observable signal**: Mother normally responsive → experimenter asks mother to freeze face for 2 minutes. Baby response progression: (Phase 1) smile/coo attempt → (Phase 2) increased effort bids → (Phase 3) **puzzled freeze + gaze avoidance** → (Phase 4) distress withdrawal/self-soothing.
- **Mechanism**: Expectation chunk "mother responds to my bids" compiled from daily experience. Violation fires error signal (ACC). Phase 3 "puzzled freeze" = PFC pattern-match failing to reconcile violation. Phase 4 = distress chunk takeover after reconciliation attempts exhaust.
- **Research base**: 🟢 Tronick 1978 original paradigm. Mesman et al. 2009 meta-analysis 500+ studies. Robust cross-culturally.
- **Confidence**: 🟢 Strongest controlled paradigm for observing PFC expectation violation in infants
- **Drill home**: `06-Social-Recognition-Arc.md` §4

### Marker 5: Baillargeon VoE longer looking

- **Age**: 4-6mo onwards (some evidence even earlier for core knowledge)
- **System**: Visual + physical reasoning
- **Observable signal**: Baby watches physical event. Impossible event (object passes through solid, etc.) produces significantly longer looking time than possible event control.
- **Mechanism**: Baby has object permanence / solidity chunk compiled (Spelke core knowledge). Impossible event violates chunk → PFC sustained attention → longer looking. Duration = PFC pattern-match load measure.
- **Research base**: 🟢🟡 Baillargeon & DeVos 1991, Spelke et al. 1992 original studies. Robust replication but some critics (Haith 1998 low-level explanation critique). Interpretation as "PFC engagement" is framework inference, not direct neural measurement.
- **Confidence**: 🟢 looking time measure / 🟡 PFC engagement interpretation
- **Drill home**: `04-Sensory-Reception-Arc.md` §4 (visual chunks for object permanence)

### Marker 6: Peek-a-boo anticipation tension (positive valence L2)

- **Age**: 10-14mo (after temporal prediction chunks compile)
- **System**: Social + temporal + multi-modal
- **Observable signal**: After ~5-10 plays, baby develops anticipatory face before reveal — slight smile building, breath held, body leaning forward. Reveal → burst of laughter.
- **Mechanism**: Temporal prediction chunk compiled from repetitions (~5-10 plays). Parent covers face → baby fires prediction chunk "reveal coming in ~1 second". Anticipation face = PFC firing prediction chunk BEFORE event occurs. Laughter at reveal = prediction confirmed reward response.
- **Research base**: 🟢 Peek-a-boo universality across cultures (Fernald & O'Neill 1993). Feel-Example E30 documents mechanism. Theme C ritual analysis (Feeling Deep-Analysis-Draft) maps ritual compile mechanism.
- **Confidence**: 🟢 Universally observable in 10-14mo babies playing peek-a-boo
- **Drill home**: `06-Social-Recognition-Arc.md` §5 (ritual + temporal prediction)

### §1.1 — Reframe summary (committed)

Per research verification conducted this session (agent report 2026-04-15), committed to framework via edits to `Imagination/PFC-Analysis.md` §4.0 + `Body-Base/Body-Parenting-Optimization.md` §0 REFRAME NOTE:

**New claim (committed)**: **PFC hardware online from prenatal**. What's missing at birth is compiled chunks (experience-dependent content), not PFC hardware. Cause of infant behavioral limitations = **P2 Chunks Base Adequacy** (H10 precondition from Body-Feedback-Draft), not PFC absence.

### §1.2 — 5 Evidence pillars (research verified)

1. **Synaptogenesis** 🟢
   - Huttenlocher 1979 (*Brain Res* 163:195-205): PFC synaptic density at newborn already at adult threshold
   - Huttenlocher & Dabholkar 1997 (*J Comp Neurol* 387:167-178): PFC peak synaptic density at ~15 months = **2x adult density**, pruning through adolescence
   - Synapse formation begins <27 weeks fetal age
   - Implication: Hardware not only present but OVER-supplied

2. **fNIRS active PFC response** 🟢
   - Grossmann 2013 (*Front Hum Neurosci* 7:340): mPFC activation at 4mo to eye contact
   - Werchan et al. 2016 (*J Neurosci* 36:10314): 8mo right dlPFC activation during hierarchical rule learning, amplitude PREDICTS learning capability
   - Farroni, Csibra, Simion & Johnson 2002 (*PNAS* 99:9602): 2-5 day old newborns prefer direct gaze, ERP evidence processing
   - Implication: PFC not silent — actively processing social + rule stimuli from first days

3. **Resting-state networks present pre-birth** 🟢
   - Doria et al. 2010 (*PNAS* 107:20015): 70 preterm infants 29-43 weeks postmenstrual age — frontoparietal + executive control + DMN networks full adult-like facsimiles by term (40 weeks)
   - Gao et al. 2009 (*PNAS* 106:6790): primitive DMN at 2-week-olds, adult-like by 2 years
   - Implication: Network architecture scaffolding present from term birth, before cognitive competencies compiled

4. **Myelination is the slow thing** 🟡
   - Yakovlev & Lecours 1967: PFC intracortical fibers myelinate last, continue through third decade
   - Implication: Signal speed develops slowly, but PFC is running throughout — just with lower signal-to-noise ratio than adult

5. **Frontal consciousness signature present from 5mo** 🟢
   - Kouider et al. 2013 (*Science* 340:376): 5mo/12mo/15mo infants show same nonlinear frontal access-consciousness signature as adult. 5mo weaker/delayed (~900ms), 12-15mo faster (~750ms), adult ~300ms. Signal "particularly prominent above frontal lobe" (P3b-like).
   - Implication: Frontal-dependent consciousness mechanism already functional at 5mo; "compiled" refines speed + amplitude

**Citation đinh reframe**: **Hodel 2018** "Rapid Infant Prefrontal Cortex Development and Sensitivity to Early Environmental Experience" (*Developmental Review* 48:113-144). Quote: *"Widespread recognition of the extended development of prefrontal cortex in human children led to the perception of prefrontal cortex as 'late developing,' resulting in an unfortunate cost: a perception of it as structurally and functionally underdeveloped in young infants and toddlers."* Paper explicitly reframes PFC = "rapidly developing region". Medial prefrontal regions "among the thickest cortex at birth".

### §1.3 — What this changes for F1

**Correct framing for F1 drill**: PFC status at newborn = **"online + active + low signal-to-noise + thiếu compiled content + thiếu myelin speed"**

| Component | Status at newborn | Develops through |
|---|---|---|
| Neurons + synapses + networks | ✅ Present (actually over-supplied) | Pruning through adolescence |
| Functional responsiveness | ✅ Present from 2-5 days (gaze) / 4mo (eye contact) / 8mo (rule learning) | Lower amplitude/slower, improves with myelination + experience |
| Myelination | 🟡 Immature | Third decade of life |
| **Compiled chunks (content)** | 🔴 **MISSING — F1 drill target** | Experience-dependent accumulation (P2 bottleneck) |
| Working memory capacity | 🔴 Limited | Chunks accumulation + integration efficiency |

**F1's analytical task**: NOT "prove PFC matures at X age". IS "document observable events across body-input systems + infer chunks state per event + validate P2 accumulation mechanism".

**Why infant cannot do adult tasks**: Not because PFC offline. Because specific chunks required for task not yet compiled. Examples:
- **"Không feel buồn đái"**: Body signal present (bladder sensors firing from prenatal). PFC able to observe (frontal consciousness signature at 5mo). Missing: chunk "bladder signal → predict urination → plan action". This chunk takes ~18-24 months to compile through repeated experience (stage A → B → C → D developmental gradient per user's Stage A/B framing).
- **"Không nghe hiểu tên"**: Auditory pathway active from fetal (26 weeks). PFC able to process sound. Missing: chunk "specific sound pattern → referent self → social response". Mandel 1995 shows 4.5mo babies ALREADY have this chunk — earlier than old "PFC immature" view would predict.
- **"Không giao tiếp bình thường với người lớn"**: Broca + Wernicke present. PFC social regions (mPFC) active from 4mo. Missing: chunks for verbal-motor production + grammar scaffolding + theory-of-mind. Gestural production at 14mo (E25) + verbal production at 18mo (E21) shows a 4-month gestural-to-verbal gap = chunks accumulation gap, not PFC gap.

## §4 — Design decisions

> Design decisions committed in v3. Each decision has rationale + precedent + alternative rejected.

### §4.1 — Body-input axis as primary organizing principle ⭐ v3 core

**Decision**: Organize F1 drill by **6 body-input systems** (visual, auditory, motor output, interoceptive, social, verbal) rather than by abstract mechanism categories (hardware, modality, pre-verbal vs verbal).

**Rationale**:
- Body-input systems are **measurable** via observable events at each developmental stage
- User's insight (§0.5 Q7): "tôi nghĩ chỉ cần tập trung vào theo hướng body-input bé cần kết nối và đọc-hiểu, điều khiển, là có thể đo lường đc sự phát triển nhận thức khá chính xác rồi nhỉ"
- Each body-input has natural arc (Connect → Read → Control) that maps to substrate chunk formation mechanism
- Events observable in each body-input give empirical grip that abstract categories lack

**Alternative rejected (v1 approach)**: Hardware → Modality → Pre-verbal → Verbal → Gap separation. Problems:
- Forced artificial separation (multi-modal binding split across hardware + modality)
- Reader must mentally recombine mechanism + data
- Pre-verbal/verbal distinction arbitrary (12mo boundary blurry)
- No clear event-to-mechanism mapping

**Precedent**: Body-Feedback-Draft N+3 organized by signal type (P1-P5 preconditions), Agent N+2 organized by mechanism type (Self-Pattern-Match / Pattern-Resonance) — both worked well as natural organizing principles.

### §4.2 — Event-inference methodology commitment ⭐ v3 core

**Decision**: F1 drill claims only what observable events plausibly require in chunks state. Does NOT claim PFC developmental timeline directly.

**Rationale** (§0.3):
- PFC cannot be directly measured in infants (no invasive scanning ethically)
- Behavioral observation is the available evidence
- Inference from events to chunks state is epistemologically defensible
- User's insight (§0.5 Q10): "câu hỏi không phải là ở giai đoạn nào có PFC. mà chúng ta chỉ cần liệt kê các event mà có khả năng có PFC"

**Operational rules**:
- Every PFC-involvement claim carries plausibility qualifier (🟡 unless direct empirical evidence 🟢)
- Every ladder level classification is about the event, not the age
- Alternative hypotheses explicitly stated + rejected with reasons
- Falsifiable predictions offered where possible

**Alternative rejected**: "PFC matures at age X" claims. Overclaim not supported by measurement. Literature often conflates behavioral failure with structural absence (see Hodel 2018 citation §1.2).

### §4.3 — PFC-Inference Ladder as analytical tool (not developmental timeline)

**Decision**: Use 5-level ladder (L0-L4) as **event classifier**, not as "PFC developmental stages".

**Rationale**: Ladder classifies events by chunks requirement. A given age has events at multiple ladder levels simultaneously. Same event climbs ladder over time as chunks accumulate. This preserves methodological commitment to event-inference.

**Operational usage**:
- Each event in arc files tagged with ladder level
- Non-uniform progression across body-inputs explicitly analyzed
- L2 "pattern-match activation" is the key empirical middle ground — observable markers here validate PFC hardware reframe

**Precedent**: User's "đơ mặt" hypothesis (§0.5 Q8-Q9) is the original L2 marker. Ladder formalizes this with 4 additional L2 examples (vaccination pre-cry, stranger anxiety face, still face Phase 3, VoE, peek-a-boo anticipation).
