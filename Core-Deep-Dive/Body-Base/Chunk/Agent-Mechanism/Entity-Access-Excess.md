```
⭐⭐ McNULTY et al. 2013 (Science, Vol. 342):

  🟢 135 newlywed couples, followed 6 months × 4 YEARS
  Method: Implicit Association Test (IAT) + explicit self-report

  KEY FINDINGS:
    ① IAT (implicit) và self-report (explicit) = NO CORRELATION
       → Spouses DID NOT KNOW their own implicit evaluations
    ② Self-report at baseline DID NOT predict satisfaction trajectory
    ③ IAT at baseline DID predict trajectory over 4 years
    ④ Negative implicit → more likely to SEPARATE within 1 year

  FRAMEWORK MAPPING:
    Self-report = PFC label (observable, schema-influenced)
    IAT = body-level evaluation (compiled, automatic, not PFC-accessible)
    → PFC: "chúng tôi hạnh phúc" (schema compliance + genuine mix)
    → Body: CÓ THỂ khác (compiled negative tags invisible to PFC)
    → = Body BIẾT, PFC KHÔNG BIẾT

  🟢 Gottman (1990s longitudinal):
    Physiological markers (heart rate, cortisol)
    PREDICTED satisfaction changes over 3 years
    → Body-level indicators MORE predictive than verbal reports

  ⭐ ENTITY-ACCESS EXCESS HIDDEN BY SELF-REPORT:

    → = WHY entity-access excess is HARD to detect and HARD to study

  ⚠️ RESEARCH GAP: NO study has tested IAT discrepancy
```
